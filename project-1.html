Project Overview:

This Microsite development project requires us to select a client, curate, and write content, define a specific group of users with a specific need, structure the content, and complete a full interactive mockup of the microsite.

Our group create a site based on the information of BC SPCA(British Columbia Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals), we aim to address the need of people that are looking for dogs to adopt. Our microsite consists of images, detailed information about the available dogs for adoption, and contact forms for further action. Our primary purpose was to create a welcoming atmosphere through design and provide direct, helpful data for our aimed user group through the content structure.

Process 1: Define User Group

We defined our user group to be young adults who are the first time adopting a dog with no prior knowledge or experiences.

Some of their needs including:

  • Be able to find an adoptable dog they like
  • Detailed description & important notices about the dog
  • Long-term companionship
  • Low-cost adoption

Process 2: Art Direction

Art direction 1

The main idea of Art direction 1 is to use interesting illustrations and bright color schemes to create a warm and welcoming atmosphere. Brings user a immersive, engaging experience.

We decide to use Simple 2D vector illustration elements throughout the website, creates a joyful and welcoming feeling for the user, also emphasize the theme of the website.

for each puppy’s information, there will be Rounded blocks and frames with high quality image attracts people’s attention and creates sense of organization, images extends outside the frame makes it looks more dynamic.

Art direction 2

Our second art direction is more focus on shape. We use vivid color like orange and green, but lower the saturation to make it feel warm-hearted.

The content block will be used to display the basic information, such as the age, characteristics and habit of the dog. The dialogue box increases a sense of interaction, improving user experience.

The image border will be rounded and irregular, so that it has a lively atmosphere. It will be suitable for young generation. And the rounded shape help create consistency of our website.

We intergated two art directions mentioned eriler in our final development of the webpage, and here's some details to notice in our work

Hand-drawn element

Hand-painted elements are added around the dog, such as hands hugging the puppy, a boy reading books with it, and showing life with a dog. Let the first-time adopters feel the beauty of the dog's company.

Rounded corners and warm colors

The rounded corners have a soft and warm feeling, with the wave-shaped block echoing the rounded corners. There is a lively feeling in the warmth, in line with the adoption theme and our user group, which is young people

Pop-up Window

It allows first-time adopters to quickly get more details about the pet and go through more pictures. Despite traditional scrolling pages, we allow users to find more pictures of dogs by pressing the button, which makes reading more efficient and suitable for young people to browse during their free time at work.

Challenges/Solutions

The most challenging part of the process is accurately targeting the user group we are looking for and coming up with a style that best suits their needs. We were first thinking about young people since they are most inclined to adopt a pet; however, it is too broad and out of focus. Then, by linking to our content, we were providing each puppy’s detailed information and notices, which we think were essential for a first-time pet owner. Then the warm and welcoming style and color palette was driven by the “first-time pet owner” user group we defined.

Also, since it was all of our group’s first time doing web development, it is also a task for us to figure out how to use web development tools like Webflow. My teammate and I did quite some research and watch many videos to try to grasp the concept of every function. As a result, we would be able to achieve most of our purposed ideations with some minor issues existing.

Reflection

I had quite a fun experience doing this Microsite web development, which I think is a successful group project. The overall presentation looks decent and almost achieved our initial concept, the style is consistent, and the content was straightforward to our user group. What I learned in this project is that finding your precise target audience is important in any of the design cases, it is the core of the design. The user group determines the content, structure, and style of the work. So it is a necessary step to do comprehensive research for the impacted user before starting any of the actual design work.
